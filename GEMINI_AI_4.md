## Detailed Version: The AI Governance & Safety Table

In this version, the **"Groups"** are based on the pillars of **Trustworthy AI**, and the **"Periods" (Rows)** represent the **Risk Tier** (from Minimal to Unacceptable).

### Group 1: Transparency & Interpretability (Tr)
* **$X_{ai}$ (Explainability):** The technical ability to explain a specific model output to a human.
* **Mc (Model Cards):** Standardized documentation of a model’s training, intent, and limitations.
* **Wm (Watermarking):** Embedding invisible markers in AI-generated content to prove its provenance.

### Group 2: Privacy & Data Sovereignty (Pv)
* **Dp (Differential Privacy):** Mathematical noise added to data to prevent individual identification.
* **Fr (Federated Learning):** Training models across decentralized devices without sharing raw data.
* **Sr (Right to be Forgotten):** The capability to "un-learn" or delete specific data points from a trained model.

### Group 3: Robustness & Security (Ro)
* **Rt (Red Teaming):** Adversarial stress-testing to find "jailbreaks" or safety bypasses.
* **Pi (Prompt Injection Defense):** Specialized filters designed to stop users from hijacking the model's instructions.
* **Ab (AI-BOM):** An "AI Bill of Materials" tracking every component, dataset, and library used in the system.

### Group 4: Fairness & Alignment (Fa)
* **Bd (Bias Detection):** Automated tools that scan for demographic or historical prejudices in outputs.
* **Al (Alignment):** Ensuring the model's goals match human values (e.g., via RLHF or DPO).
* **Hc (Human-in-the-loop):** A mandatory manual check for high-stakes decisions (like hiring or medical diagnosis).
