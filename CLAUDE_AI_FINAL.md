# The Unified AI Periodic Table
## A Combined Master Plan

**Version:** March 2026 Edition
**Sources synthesized:** GROK Idea 1 (Historical Evolution), GROK Idea 5 (Emergent Capabilities & Responsibility), OpenAI Idea 2 (Capability Spectrum), OpenAI Idea 3 (LLM Lifecycle), Claude Idea 3 (Capability Maturity), Claude Idea 5 (Risks & Safeguards Dual Table)

---

## 1. The Core Thesis

No single table captures AI. The real periodic table works because chemistry has one organizing axis — atomic number — that predicts everything else. AI has no single axis. It has at least four: **what it can do** (capabilities), **how it was built** (techniques and history), **how it operates over time** (lifecycle), and **what it unleashes** (risks and safeguards). The six source documents each nail one or two of these axes. This plan fuses them into a layered system — one artifact with multiple reading modes, like a map that can show topography, roads, weather, and political borders depending on which overlay you select.

The unified design is called **The AI Periodic System** (not just "table") because it is a system of interlocking views, not a single grid.

---

## 2. Architecture: Four Layers, One Artifact

The system has four layers. The first is the primary visual. The other three are overlays that can be toggled on in the interactive version or printed as companion panels alongside the poster.

### Layer 1 — THE CAPABILITY TABLE (Primary Grid)
*What AI can do, and how mature each capability is today.*

This is the "home screen" — the grid a viewer sees first. It draws its structure from **Claude Idea 3** (the strongest capability taxonomy and maturity scoring) and its column logic from **OpenAI Idea 2** (behavioral families progressing from sensing to acting).

### Layer 2 — THE HISTORICAL SPINE (Timeline Overlay)
*When each element emerged, which era it belongs to, and where it's headed.*

Drawn from **GROK Idea 1**. Instead of replacing the capability grid, history is encoded *inside* each element tile (year of origin, era color-band, half-life estimate) and as a togglable overlay that recolors the grid by era.

### Layer 3 — THE LIFECYCLE LOOP (Operations Overlay)
*How AI systems are built, deployed, monitored, and evolved over time.*

Drawn from **OpenAI Idea 3**. This is not a separate table — it's a circular diagram that maps each capability element to the lifecycle phase(s) it participates in, showing the closed-loop nature of production AI.

### Layer 4 — THE RISK / SAFEGUARD DIPTYCH (Impact Overlay)
*What can go wrong, what protects us, and where the gaps are.*

Drawn from **Claude Idea 5** and **GROK Idea 5**. A mirrored dual-panel that pairs every major risk with its countermeasures, using the capability table's elements as anchor points.

## 3. Layer 1 — The Capability Table (Detailed Design)

### 3.1 Columns: Eight Capability Families

Arranged left-to-right along a cognitive arc from perceiving the world to acting in it. This progression is borrowed from OpenAI Idea 2's P→L→K→R→C→A→S flow, expanded with Claude Idea 3's richer categories.

| Col | Family | Icon | Core Question | Color |
|-----|--------|------|---------------|-------|
| **P** | Perception | Eye | "What is there?" | Sky Blue |
| **U** | Understanding | Magnifying glass | "What does it mean?" | Teal |
| **R** | Reasoning | Lightbulb | "What should I conclude?" | Indigo |
| **G** | Generation | Paintbrush | "What can I create?" | Crimson |
| **M** | Memory & Retrieval | Archive | "What do I know?" | Amber |
| **I** | Interaction | Speech bubble | "How do I engage?" | Green |
| **J** | Judgment & Trust | Shield | "Should I believe this?" | Gold |
| **A** | Autonomy & Agency | Robot arm | "Can it act alone?" | Slate |

This creates a natural left-to-right gradient: capabilities on the left (Perception) are more solved and commoditized; capabilities on the right (Autonomy) are more frontier and more dangerous. That gradient is itself a key insight the table communicates at a glance.

### 3.2 Rows: Five Maturity Levels

Borrowed directly from Claude Idea 3's M1–M5 system, which is the cleanest and most empirically grounded scoring across all six sources.

| Row | Label | Meaning |
|-----|-------|---------|
| **M5** | Solved | Superhuman or reliably human-level in production. Commoditized. |
| **M4** | Strong | Works well in most contexts. Deployed at scale. |
| **M3** | Emerging | Impressive demos, works in constrained settings, brittle at edges. |
| **M2** | Early | Research frontier. Promising papers, not reliable in production. |
| **M1** | Nascent | Proof-of-concept only. Community agrees it matters, nobody's cracked it. |

Elements are placed at the intersection of their capability family (column) and their current maturity (row). The grid naturally fills out with M5 elements concentrated in the upper-left (Perception, Understanding) and M1 elements concentrated in the lower-right (Autonomy, Judgment) — visually encoding the field's overall shape.

### 3.3 Element Tile Design

Each tile is a dense information card. This borrows the best properties from all six sources:

```
┌──────────────────────────────┐
│ M4  ↑         Cg    [2021]   │  ← Maturity, Velocity, Symbol, Year
│                              │
│     Code Generation          │  ← Full name
│                              │
│   ████████░░  [4/5]          │  ← Progress bar
│                              │
│  ⚗ R3+Tu+Cg → Aa            │  ← Bond formula (how it combines)
│  ⏱ 10y+   ⚖ U:9 R:4         │  ← Half-life, Utility/Risk dual score
│  ● ● ○                      │  ← Cross-reference dots (other families)
│  GENERATION          DL/FM   │  ← Family label, Era tag
└──────────────────────────────┘
```

**Property breakdown:**

| Property | Source Idea | Purpose |
|----------|------------|---------|
| **Symbol** (2–4 chars) | GROK 1, all | Quick identification. Pronounceable, memorable. |
| **Maturity score** (M1–M5) | Claude 3 | How mature the capability is today. |
| **Velocity arrow** (↑ ↗ →) | Claude 3 | Direction of improvement. ↑ = rapid, ↗ = steady, → = plateau. |
| **Year** | GROK 1 | First influential paper or breakthrough adoption year. |
| **Progress bar** | Claude 3 | Visual shorthand for maturity — enables instant scanning. |
| **Bond formula** | OpenAI 2 | How this element combines with others to form systems (the "capability equation"). |
| **Half-life** | OpenAI 3, GROK 1 | Estimated remaining relevance in years. |
| **Dual score (Utility / Risk)** | GROK 5 | Two 0–10 scores showing both benefit and danger. |
| **Cross-reference dots** | Claude 3 | Colored dots showing which other families this capability depends on. |
| **Era tag** | GROK 1 | Which historical period this element emerged in (Symbolic, Classical ML, Deep Learning, Foundation Models, Agentic). |

### 3.4 The Undiscovered Elements Zone

From Claude Idea 3 — the single most compelling design feature across all six documents. A dashed-border band below the main grid, styled like a blueprint/wireframe, containing predicted capabilities that no system demonstrates even at M1. These are the Mendeleev gaps.

**Selected undiscovered elements:**

| Sym | Predicted Capability | Expected Family |
|-----|---------------------|-----------------|
| **?1** | True Common Sense | Reasoning |
| **?2** | Lossless Lifelong Learning | Memory & Retrieval |
| **?3** | Cross-Domain Creative Transfer | Generation |
| **?4** | Genuine Surprise & Curiosity | Autonomy & Agency |
| **?5** | Cultural & Contextual Fluency | Understanding |
| **?6** | Robust Physical Intuition | Reasoning |
| **?7** | Autonomous Ethical Reasoning | Judgment & Trust |

Their presence is the table's strongest intellectual claim: the field knows where it has holes.

### 3.5 Capability Bonds — The Breakthrough Concept

From OpenAI Idea 2. Capabilities don't exist alone — they combine like molecules. This is encoded in each tile's bond formula and expanded in a companion "Reaction Guide."

**Key equations:**

```
K2 (Retrieval) + L3 (Generation) → RAG System
L4 (Dialogue) + R3 (Planning) + A1 (Tool Use) → Agent
R4 (Strategic Reasoning) + K4 (Long-term Memory) + A4 (Autonomous Workflow) → Multi-Agent Swarm
P3 (Multimodal Perception) + G3 (Video Gen) + R3 (Planning) → World Model
```

This creates a compositional language for describing AI architectures. A startup can say "We're building an M3-level R+K+A system" and be precisely understood.

## 4. Layer 2 — The Historical Spine (Timeline Overlay)

From GROK Idea 1. When the historical overlay is toggled on, the grid's background recolors by era, and each element's era tag becomes its dominant visual feature.

### 4.1 Seven Eras

| Period | Era | Years | Color |
|--------|-----|-------|-------|
| 1 | Symbolic / GOFAI | ~1950s–1980s | Cool blue-gray |
| 2 | Connectionism & Early Neural Nets | ~1980s–1990s | Steel blue |
| 3 | Statistical / Classical ML | ~1995–2010 | Sage green |
| 4 | Deep Learning Revolution | ~2012–2018 | Electric blue |
| 5 | Foundation Models & Scaling | ~2018–2023 | Warm amber |
| 6 | Post-Training & Alignment | ~2023–2025 | Orange |
| 7 | Agentic / Multimodal / Reasoning | 2025–present | Vivid purple |

The progressive color warmth (blue-gray → vivid purple) encodes acceleration at a glance.

### 4.2 Special Historical Blocks

From GROK Idea 1's lanthanide/actinide analogy:

**"Revivals & Hybrids" inset** (bottom-left): Techniques that died and came back stronger — neuro-symbolic reasoning, decision trees → XGBoost → TabPFN, diffusion + transformers. This block tells the story of AI's cyclical nature.

**"Speculative / Frontier" inset** (bottom-right): Emerging or hypothetical elements — liquid neural nets, SSMs beyond Mamba, scalable oversight primitives, mechanistic interpretability tools.

**"Isotopes" panel**: Major variants of popular elements. GPT isotopes (GPT-2 through GPT-4o, o1, o3). Llama isotopes (2 through 4). Claude isotopes. This acknowledges that "Transformer" is not one thing — it's a family with distinct generations.

### 4.3 Reactivity Indicator

From GROK Idea 1. Each element carries a reactivity color showing how well it combines with modern systems:

- **Green**: Frequently combined with current LLMs/agents (e.g., attention mechanisms, RLHF)
- **Yellow**: Still useful but niche (e.g., SVMs for specific tabular tasks)
- **Red**: Mostly historical / replaced (e.g., hand-crafted feature engineering)

## 5. Layer 3 — The Lifecycle Loop (Operations Overlay)

From OpenAI Idea 3. This layer addresses the dimension none of the capability or history views capture: AI systems are not static. They are built, deployed, monitored, degraded, and rebuilt in a continuous cycle.

### 5.1 Six Lifecycle Phases (The Loop)

```
    ┌─── TRAINING ───┐
    │                 │
EVOLUTION          ALIGNMENT
    │                 │
OPERATIONS ←── DEPLOYMENT
    │
GOVERNANCE (overlays everything)
```

This is a closed loop. Each "cycle" represents one generation of an AI system's evolution.

| Phase | Symbol | Core Question |
|-------|--------|---------------|
| **Training** | T | "How is intelligence created?" |
| **Alignment** | Al | "Is it behaving correctly?" |
| **Deployment** | D | "How is it delivered?" |
| **Operations** | O | "Is it working reliably?" |
| **Evolution** | E | "How does it improve?" |
| **Governance** | Gv | "Is it safe and compliant?" |

### 5.2 Lifecycle Elements

Each phase contains its own elements with a unique property — **half-life** — measuring how quickly the element becomes outdated:

| Element | Phase | Half-life |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| Prompt engineering | Evolution | Weeks–months |
| Fine-tuning | Evolution | Months |
| RAG index updates | Evolution | Weeks |
| Pretraining | Training | Years |
| Safety filters | Alignment | Months (arms race) |
| Governance frameworks | Governance | Years–decades |

Half-life is the key insight here: some parts of AI systems decay quickly (prompts, filters), while others are stable infrastructure (pretraining, governance). This informs investment: short-half-life elements need continuous operational attention; long-half-life elements need upfront strategic investment.

### 5.3 Failure Modes per Phase

From OpenAI Idea 3's "toxic factors" concept (paralleling the SEO table's toxins):

| Phase | Primary Failure Mode |
|-------|---------------------|
| Training | Poisoned or biased data |
| Alignment | Sycophancy, reward hacking |
| Deployment | Latency, cost explosion |
| Operations | Silent drift, undetected degradation |
| Evolution | Overfitting, capability regression |
| Governance | Regulatory breach, accountability gaps |

### 5.4 Bridge to Layer 1

Every capability element in the primary grid maps to lifecycle phases. For example:

| Capability | Lifecycle Mapping |
|-----------|-------------------|
| RAG | Training (data indexing) → Deployment (retrieval infra) → Operations (index freshness) → Evolution (re-indexing) |
| Agents | Alignment (tool-use safety) → Deployment (orchestration) → Operations (monitoring loops) → Governance (audit trails) |
| Code Generation | Training (code corpora) → Alignment (security eval) → Deployment (IDE integration) → Evolution (feedback-driven improvement) |

This lets practitioners move from "what AI does" (Layer 1) to "how to keep it running" (Layer 3).

## 6. Layer 4 — The Risk / Safeguard Diptych (Impact Overlay)

From Claude Idea 5 (the most detailed and urgent design) and GROK Idea 5 (the dual-charge concept). This is the most important layer to build, even though it's the hardest to get right.

### 6.1 Structure: Mirrored Dual Panels

Two panels sharing a central spine:

- **Left panel — Safeguards**: Elements that protect. Green-tinted tiles on warm-neutral background.
- **Right panel — Risks**: Elements that threaten. Red-tinted tiles on cool-neutral background.
- **Central spine — Pairing diagrams**: Miniature network graphs showing which safeguards counter which risks.

### 6.2 Six Risk/Safeguard Domains

Both panels are organized by domain (horizontal bands):

| Domain | Color | Focus |
|--------|-------|-------|
| 1. Alignment & Intent | Deep Blue | Ensuring AI pursues human goals |
| 2. Data Integrity & Privacy | Forest Green | Protecting data quality and legality |
| 3. Robustness & Security | Crimson | Defending against attacks and manipulation |
| 4. Fairness & Inclusion | Amber | Equitable treatment across groups |
| 5. Transparency & Accountability | Violet | Making systems auditable and answerable |
| 6. Systemic & Societal | Slate Gray | Large-scale economic, political, existential risks |

### 6.3 Dual Scoring System

From Claude Idea 5's two-axis approach, which is far richer than a single +/- scale:

**For Risks:**
- **Severity (S1–S5)**: How much damage at worst. S5 = civilization-scale.
- **Maturity (M1–M5)**: How developed the attack vector is today. M5 = actively happening.

**For Safeguards:**
- **Value (V1–V5)**: How important if fully implemented. V5 = foundational.
- **Deployment (D1–D5)**: How widely deployed today. D5 = industry standard.

This creates the four critical quadrants:

|  | High Severity/Value | Low Severity/Value |
|--|--------------------|--------------------|
| **High Maturity/Deployment** | Active battlefield | Solved or negligible |
| **Low Maturity/Deployment** | **Ticking time bomb / Urgent R&D gap** | Horizon scan |

The upper-left quadrant (high severity + low deployment) is the most actionable signal — it shows where the field knows what to do but almost nobody is doing it.

### 6.4 Key Risk–Safeguard Pairs (Selected)

| Risk | S | M | ←→ | Safeguard | V | D |
|------|---|---|-----|-----------|---|---|
| Prompt Injection | S5 | M5 | ←→ | Input Filtering + Prompt Isolation | V5 | D3 |
| Goal Misgeneralization | S5 | M2 | ←→ | Interpretability + Constitutional AI | V5 | D2 |
| Deceptive Alignment | S5 | M1 | ←→ | Formal Verification + Debate | V4 | D2 |
| Data Poisoning | S5 | M4 | ←→ | Deduplication + Provenance Tracking | V5 | D3 |
| Discriminatory Performance | S5 | M4 | ←→ | Bias Auditing + Fairness-Aware Training | V5 | D3 |
| Concentration of Power | S5 | M3 | ←→ | Regulation + Open-Source Ecosystem | V5 | D2 |
| Job Displacement | S4 | M4 | ←→ | Economic Transition Planning | V4 | D2 |

### 6.5 Orphan Elements

From Claude Idea 5 — the most intellectually honest section of the entire system.

**Risks without clear safeguards:**
- Meaning & Purpose Erosion (if AI does most cognitive work, what gives human effort meaning?)
- Relational Displacement (AI companions weakening social bonds)
- Unknown Unknowns (this tile exists solely to maintain humility)

**Safeguards without clear risks:**
- Positive-Sum AI Design (a philosophy, not a countermeasure)
- Dignity-Preserving AI (a values commitment, not a technical fix)

### 6.6 Coverage Heat Map

At the top of the central spine, a summary bar shows overall "defense coverage" per domain — the ratio of average safeguard deployment to average risk maturity. Red domains = we're losing. Green = keeping pace. In March 2026: Robustness & Security glows red (prompt injection at M5 vs. defenses at D3). Transparency is yellow. Systemic/Societal is deep red.

## 8. Periodic Trends (Cross-Cutting Patterns)

These trends hold across the entire system, giving it the predictive power of real periodic trends:

### Left → Right (Perception → Autonomy)
- ↑ Human-centric complexity
- ↑ Subjectivity and ambiguity
- ↑ Risk and alignment importance
- ↓ Maturity and commoditization

### Top → Bottom (M5 → M1)
- ↑ Generality and adaptiveness
- ↑ Research intensity
- ↑ Value creation potential
- ↑ Risk if deployed prematurely

### Past → Present (Era 1 → Era 7)
- ↑ Scale (parameters, data, compute)
- ↑ Emergent behaviors
- ↑ Combination complexity (more bonds per element)
- ↓ Interpretability

### Lifecycle Phase → Phase (T → E)
- ↑ Operational complexity
- ↑ Organizational involvement
- ↑ Number of stakeholders
- ↓ Individual technical control

## 9. Formats & Deliverables

### 9.1 Printable Poster (Primary)
- **Layer 1 only**: Portrait 24"×36" poster. The capability grid with maturity bars, velocity arrows, and undiscovered zone. Office walls, classrooms, conference booths.
- **Full system**: Landscape 36"×48" poster with all four layers. Layer 1 as the central grid, Layer 2 as era color-coding, Layer 4 as flanking diptych panels. Layer 3 as a circular inset diagram.

### 9.2 Interactive Web Version
- Default view: Layer 1 capability grid.
- Toggle buttons for each overlay (History, Lifecycle, Risk/Safeguard).
- Click any tile to expand: full description, key papers, bond recipes, risk pairings, lifecycle mapping.
- Filter by maturity, velocity, era, family, risk severity.
- "Time machine" slider to compare annual editions.
- "Coverage overlay" that highlights worst-defended risks.

### 9.3 Workshop / Audit Tool
From GROK Idea 5's workshop mode. Teams score their own systems against the table:
- "Which capability elements does our product rely on?"
- "What maturity level are we actually operating at?"
- "Which lifecycle phases are we weakest in?"
- "What risk pairings have we left unaddressed?"
- Gap analysis output: a report showing coverage, blind spots, and priority investments.

### 9.4 Capability Scoring Engine
From OpenAI Idea 2's most strategic suggestion. The bond equations and maturity scores become a classification system for AI companies and products:
- "OpenAI is strong in G, R, A (Generation, Reasoning, Autonomy)"
- "Anthropic is strong in J, A (Judgment, Alignment + Agency)"
- "Tesla is strong in P, A (Perception, Action)"
- Portfolio analysis: map investment exposure across the capability grid.

### 9.5 Living Repository
GitHub repo for community contributions: new elements (with year, paper link, proposed symbol), maturity score updates, bond recipe submissions. The table evolves like the real periodic table, with annual "edition" snapshots.

## 10. Design Principles

These govern every design decision across all layers:

1. **Scannable at 10 feet, readable at 2 feet.** The poster works as a wall decoration (color patterns communicate the field's shape) and as a reference document (individual tiles carry dense information).

2. **Honest about uncertainty.** Undiscovered elements, orphan risks, velocity arrows, and maturity scores with version dates all signal that this is a living snapshot, not a timeless truth.

3. **Audience-adaptive.** Layer 1 serves everyone (the "newspaper version"). Layer 3 serves operators and engineers. Layer 4 serves policymakers, risk officers, and executives. The same artifact speaks to all audiences through different reading modes.

4. **Faithful to the periodic table metaphor.** Elements have symbols, groups, periods, and trends. Bonds create compounds. Some elements are stable, others radioactive. There are undiscovered slots. The metaphor is not decorative — it's structural.

5. **Versioned and dateable.** Every edition carries a prominent date. Archived editions become a visual record of the field's progress — a time-lapse no single paper can provide.

6. **Not a sales deck.** The undiscovered elements, the orphan risks, the M1 and M2 scores, and the red-glowing coverage bars all admit what AI cannot do. A marketing artifact never admits weakness. This one does, and that's what makes it credible.

## 11. What Makes This Plan Different From Any One Source

| Dimension | Best single source | What the unified plan adds |
|-----------|-------------------|---------------------------|
| Capability taxonomy | Claude Idea 3 | Adds bond equations (OpenAI 2), dual utility/risk scores (GROK 5), and lifecycle mapping (OpenAI 3) to each tile |
| Historical context | GROK Idea 1 | Encodes history inside capability tiles rather than replacing the grid — history becomes a lens, not a competing structure |
| Operational relevance | OpenAI Idea 3 | Connects the lifecycle loop to specific capability elements rather than standing alone as a separate framework |
| Risk communication | Claude Idea 5 | Anchors every risk and safeguard to specific capabilities in the main grid, so the diptych isn't floating in abstract space |
| Audience breadth | Claude Idea 3 (public-facing) | Four reading modes serve four audiences (general public, engineers, operators, policymakers) from one artifact |
| Forward-looking power | GROK Idea 5, Claude Idea 3 | Undiscovered elements + orphan risks + speculative frontier block combine into a comprehensive map of what's missing |

The key synthesis move is that **no layer stands alone**. Every risk in Layer 4 maps to a capability in Layer 1. Every lifecycle phase in Layer 3 touches specific elements in Layer 1. Every historical era in Layer 2 colors specific tiles in Layer 1. The capability grid is the anchor, and everything else is a view into it.
