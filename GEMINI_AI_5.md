Idea 5, the **"Intelligence & Capabilities" Table**, is the "User’s Edition." While the other tables focus on how the "sausage is made" or how to keep the factory safe, this model focuses entirely on the **output**. 

In 2026, we’ve moved past asking "Which model are you using?" and started asking "What can your AI actually *do*?" This table treats intelligence as a set of modular building blocks that can be combined to solve specific human problems.

---

## Evaluation of Idea 5: The Functional Model

* **Strengths:** It is the most "future-proof" model. Whether an AI uses a Transformer architecture or a future "Neuro-symbolic" breakthrough, the capability (e.g., **Translation**) remains the same. It’s perfect for non-technical stakeholders who need to assemble an AI solution like Legos.
* **Weaknesses:** It can be overly broad. Because modern LLMs are "generalists," they technically possess almost every element in this table at once, making the "separation" of elements feel artificial.
* **Best Use Case:** Product discovery, defining "Job-to-be-Done" (JTBD), and comparing the utility of different AI agents.

## Detailed Version: The AI Capabilities Periodic Table

In this version, elements are grouped by **Cognitive Function**. The **"Atomic Number"** represents the complexity of the task, and the **"Group"** represents the domain of intelligence.

### Group 1: Perception & Sensing (Pe)
How the AI "sees" and "hears" the world.
* **$V_i$ (Vision):** 01. Identifying objects, faces, and text within images.
* **$S_t$ (Speech-to-Text):** 02. Transcribing spoken language with high fidelity.
* **$S_e$ (Sentiment):** 03. Detecting the emotional tone behind a piece of content.
* **$O_c$ (OCR):** 04. Extracting structured data from messy physical documents.

### Group 2: Reasoning & Knowledge (Re)
The "Internal Logic" used to process information.
* **$Su$ (Summarization):** 05. Distilling massive datasets into core insights.
* **$Ma$ (Mathematics):** 06. Solving complex symbolic or numerical problems.
* **$Co$ (Coding):** 07. Generating, debugging, and explaining programming languages.
* **$Lo$ (Logic):** 08. Chain-of-thought reasoning to navigate multi-step problems.

### Group 3: Creative Expression (Cr)
Generating new "atoms" of content from scratch.
* **$Im$ (Image Gen):** 09. Creating high-fidelity visuals from text prompts.
* **$Vg$ (Video Gen):** 10. Generating temporal sequences (video) with physics-aware consistency.
* **$Mu$ (Music/Audio):** 11. Composing original audio tracks or realistic voice clones.
* **$Tr$ (Translation):** 12. Moving meaning across linguistic and cultural barriers.

### Group 4: Agency & Interaction (Ag)
The AI’s ability to "step out" of the chat box and do work.
* **$Br$ (Browsing):** 13. Navigating the live web to find real-time info.
* **$Ap$ (API Use):** 14. Interacting with other software (e.g., booking a flight, sending an email).
* **$Pl$ (Planning):** 15. Breaking a long-term goal into a series of executable sub-tasks.
* **$Me$ (Memory):** 16. Long-term "Personalization" that remembers user preferences over months.

## The "Multimodal" Reaction
In this table, the most powerful "compounds" are created by combining elements from different groups. 

| Compound | Formula | Real-World Use Case |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **The Analyst** | $V_i + Ma + Su$ | Analyzing a photo of a financial spreadsheet and summarizing trends. |
| **The Developer** | $Co + Br + Lo$ | Researching a new API documentation and writing the integration code. |
| **The Director** | $Vg + Mu + Pe$ | Generating a movie scene with a specific emotional soundtrack. |

---

## The "Emergence" Scale (Atomic Weight)
The **Atomic Weight** here measures "Emergent Intelligence." 
* **Light Elements (1.0 - 10.0):** Narrow AI tasks like $O_c$ (OCR) or $S_t$ (Speech-to-Text).
* **Heavy Elements (50.0+):** High-reasoning tasks like $Pl$ (Planning) and $Lo$ (Logic) which require massive compute and sophisticated "brain" power.

> **Final Insight:** This table is the "Menu" for the future of work. When you build an "AI Agent," you are essentially picking 3–5 elements from this table and binding them together into a stable "molecular" employee.
